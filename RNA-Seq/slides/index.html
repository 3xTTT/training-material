Benefits ![](../images/wordcloud.png) --- ## How to analyze RNA seq data? --- ### FASTQ-files ``` @4:1:4888:1039:Y TGAACGCTGTTTCCAAGAAATGCTGGAAGAGGTCGATGGGTGTTATCTCTG + IIIHIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIH!CBCBBBB@=B@A?1@==<@= ``` - 50+ million reads per data set (~15 GB data for one human fastq-file) - Unique identifier per read - Nucleotide sequence with single base resolution - Quality score for each base --- ### Data Processing - Overview ![](../images/RNA_seq_processing.png) - Currently no standardized workflow available - Every dataset can have multiple best practices --- ### Data Pre-processing - Adapter clipping to trim the sequencing adapters - Quality trimming to remove wrongly called bases and those with a low quality - Please, see [NGS Quality control](../../NGS-QC/slides/index.html) for further information --- ### RNA-seq Workflows and Tools ![](../images/turner_2013.png) [*Turner, 2013*](https://figshare.com/articles/RNA_seq_Workflows_and_Tools/662782) --- ### Genomic Alignment ![](/images/RNA_seq_conesa2016.png) [*Conesa et al, Genome Biol, 2016*](https://genomebiology.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s13059-016-0881-8) --- ### Normalization -
